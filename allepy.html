Alleppey Backwaters - Kerala

About:

The **Alleppey Backwaters**, located in the heart of **Kerala**, are a network of tranquil lagoons, lakes, and canals. Known as the **Venice of the East**, Alleppey (Alappuzha) offers a unique experience of floating through lush green landscapes in traditional houseboats, called **Kettuvallams**.

The serene backwaters, surrounded by **paddy fields**, **coconut groves**, and **village life**, make Alleppey a top tourist destination in **Kerala** and one of India's most enchanting natural wonders.

Top Attractions:

  • Houseboat cruises offering luxurious stays and authentic Kerala cuisine.
  • Alleppey Beach and **Alappuzha Lighthouse**.
  • The annual **Nehru Trophy Boat Race**, held in August.
  • Visit nearby villages to experience traditional Kerala life.

Things to Do:

  • Book a **houseboat cruise** for a day or overnight stay.
  • Explore the **village canals** by small boat or kayak.
  • Enjoy fresh seafood at floating restaurants.
  • Witness stunning sunsets over the backwaters.

Location: Alappuzha (Alleppey), Kerala

Best Time to Visit: October - March (Pleasant weather)

Visitor Tips:

  • Book your **houseboat** in advance during peak season.
  • Carry mosquito repellent for evening cruises.
  • Try traditional Kerala dishes like **Karimeen Fry** and **Puttu Kadala**.
  • Respect the local culture and natural beauty by avoiding littering.
