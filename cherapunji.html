Cherrapunji - Meghalaya

About:

**Cherrapunji** (also called Sohra) is a breathtaking hill station located in the **East Khasi Hills** district of **Meghalaya**. It is known for being one of the **wettest places on Earth**, receiving **heavy rainfall** throughout the year. The region is famous for its lush green landscapes, spectacular waterfalls, and **living root bridges** built by the indigenous Khasi people.

The misty hills, **caves**, and verdant valleys of Cherrapunji make it a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

Top Attractions:

  • **Nohkalikai Falls** – India’s tallest plunge waterfall.
  • **Mawsmai Cave** – A beautiful limestone cave for adventure lovers.
  • **Living Root Bridges** – Unique bridges grown naturally over decades.
  • **Seven Sisters Falls** – Magnificent seven-segment waterfall.

Things to Do:

  • Explore **root bridges** through trekking trails.
  • Visit viewpoints offering **panoramic valley views**.
  • Experience the unique culture of the **Khasi tribes**.
  • Enjoy the scenic drives through misty roads.

Location: East Khasi Hills, Meghalaya

Best Time to Visit: October - May (Avoid heavy monsoon months for better trekking conditions)

Visitor Tips:

  • Carry raincoats and waterproof gear.
  • Be prepared for sudden changes in weather.
  • Visit early morning for **clearer views of waterfalls**.
  • Respect the local **traditions and environment**.
