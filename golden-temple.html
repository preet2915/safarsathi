Golden Temple - Punjab

About:

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is the most iconic Sikh gurdwara in the world. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, it serves as a central religious place for Sikhs. The temple's architecture is a blend of Hindu and Islamic styles, and its golden dome reflects beautifully in the Amrit Sarovar (sacred pool) that surrounds it.

The temple welcomes visitors of all faiths and is known for its spiritual ambiance, stunning beauty, and the **Langar** â€” a free community kitchen that serves meals to thousands daily regardless of caste, creed, or religion.

History:

Construction of the Golden Temple began in 1581 under the supervision of Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru. The temple has been rebuilt and renovated multiple times over the centuries, with the gold plating added during the reign of Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the early 19th century.

Key Features:

  • Magnificent gold-plated dome and structure
  • 24-hour **Kirtan** (spiritual music) inside the temple
  • The holy **Amrit Sarovar** (sacred pool) around the temple
  • World's largest free community kitchen (Langar)
  • Spiritual and peaceful environment

Location: Amritsar, Punjab

Best Time to Visit: October - March (Pleasant weather)

Special Occasions: Visit during **Gurpurab** or **Vaisakhi** to witness grand celebrations.

Visitor Tips:

  • Cover your head before entering the temple.
  • Remove shoes and wash your feet before entry.
  • Photography inside the sanctum is prohibited.
  • Maintain silence and respect the spiritual atmosphere.
