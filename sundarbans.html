Sundarbans - West Bengal

About:

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest mangrove forest in the world. Spanning across India and Bangladesh, the Indian side of the Sundarbans lies in the state of West Bengal. It is famous for its dense forests, unique biodiversity, and as the home to the **Royal Bengal Tiger**.

The Sundarbans' landscape is a mosaic of tidal rivers, mudflats, and small islands. It plays a crucial role in protecting the coastal areas from storms and acts as a natural carbon sink.

Flora & Fauna:

  • Royal Bengal Tiger
  • Saltwater Crocodiles
  • Irrawaddy Dolphins
  • King Cobras
  • Unique Mangrove Species like Sundari Tree

Unique Experiences:

  • Boat Safaris through the mangrove creeks
  • Spotting tigers swimming between islands
  • Visiting local villages and learning about their eco-friendly lifestyle
  • Exploring Sajnekhali Watchtower and the Mangrove Interpretation Centre

Location: South 24 Parganas, West Bengal

Best Time to Visit: October - March (pleasant weather and higher chances of wildlife spotting)

Visitor Tips:

  • Opt for government-registered boat safaris for safety.
  • Carry insect repellents and sun protection.
  • Respect wildlife and avoid feeding animals.
  • Explore eco-tourism lodges for sustainable stays.
