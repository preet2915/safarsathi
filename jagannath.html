Jagannath Temple - Odisha

About:

The **Jagannath Temple** in **Puri, Odisha**, is one of the **Char Dham** pilgrimage sites for Hindus and a spiritual landmark of India. Dedicated to **Lord Jagannath**, a form of Lord Vishnu, the temple is famous for its annual **Rath Yatra** (Chariot Festival), which draws millions of devotees from across the world.

Constructed in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva, the temple stands as a magnificent example of **Kalinga architecture**, with its towering shikhara (spire) visible from miles away.

Architectural Highlights:

  • Massive 214-foot high shikhara (spire).
  • Intricate stone carvings on the temple walls.
  • Distinct inner chambers for Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra.
  • The temple's **Mahaprasad**, considered sacred and cooked using age-old techniques.

Things to Do:

  • Attend the **Rath Yatra** if visiting in July.
  • Explore the bustling lanes around the temple selling religious souvenirs and Odia sweets.
  • Visit the nearby **Puri Beach** for a relaxing evening.
  • Experience the temple's **Mahaprasad**, served at the Anand Bazaar inside the premises.

Location: Puri, Odisha

Best Time to Visit: October - March (pleasant weather); July for **Rath Yatra**.

Visitor Tips:

  • Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the main sanctum, but they can view the temple from **Raghunandan Library’s rooftop**.
  • Photography inside the temple premises is strictly prohibited.
  • Dress modestly as it is a sacred religious site.
  • Hire a licensed guide to understand the temple’s rich history and rituals.
