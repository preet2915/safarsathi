Lotus Temple - Delhi

About:

The **Lotus Temple**, located in New Delhi, is one of the most iconic landmarks of India, known for its unique lotus-shaped design. Completed in 1986, it serves as a **Bahá'í House of Worship**, welcoming people of all faiths to meditate and pray in silence.

The temple's serene ambiance, stunning architecture, and lush gardens attract millions of visitors each year, making it one of Delhi’s top tourist attractions.

Architectural Highlights:

  • Shaped like a fully-bloomed **lotus flower** with 27 marble petals.
  • Surrounded by **nine pools**, enhancing its beauty.
  • Built from pristine white marble sourced from Greece.
  • Open to people of all religions for silent prayer and meditation.

Things to Do:

  • Experience the tranquil atmosphere inside the temple.
  • Walk around the beautifully landscaped **gardens**.
  • Learn about the **Bahá'í faith** and the temple’s significance.
  • Visit nearby attractions such as **ISKCON Temple** and **Humayun’s Tomb**.

Location: New Delhi, Delhi

Best Time to Visit: October - March (pleasant weather)

Visitor Tips:

  • Maintain silence inside the prayer hall.
  • Photography is allowed outside but not inside the temple hall.
  • Entry is free, but large bags and shoes must be deposited outside.
  • Visit early morning or evening for fewer crowds and better photos.
