Red Fort - Delhi

About:

The **Red Fort**, or **Lal Qila**, is one of Indiaâ€™s most iconic monuments and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by **Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan** in 1648, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

The fort, made of stunning red sandstone, stands as a symbol of India's rich history and cultural heritage. Every year, the **Prime Minister of India** delivers the Independence Day speech from the ramparts of the Red Fort.

Architectural Highlights:

  • Massive red sandstone walls over 30 meters high
  • Intricate Mughal architecture with Persian influences
  • Beautifully landscaped gardens and water channels
  • Structures within the fort: **Diwan-i-Aam**, **Diwan-i-Khas**, and **Rang Mahal**

Things to Do:

  • Explore the **Lahori Gate**, the main entrance.
  • Visit the **Mumtaz Mahal Museum** inside the fort.
  • Enjoy the **Sound and Light Show** held in the evenings.
  • Walk along the ramparts for stunning views of Old Delhi.

Location: Old Delhi, Delhi

Best Time to Visit: October - March (pleasant weather)

Visitor Tips:

  • Visit early to avoid crowds, especially on weekends.
  • Photography is allowed, but some areas may have restrictions.
  • Explore nearby attractions like **Jama Masjid** and **Chandni Chowk**.
  • Book tickets online during peak tourist season.
